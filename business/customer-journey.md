# Customer Journey Map for Scope Shield

## Aware
- **Trigger Event:** Open source developers and maintainers experience scope creep in their projects, leading to burnout.
- **Friction Points:** Lack of awareness about tools that can help manage project scope; existing tools are not tailored for open source projects.
- **User Emotions:** Frustration, overwhelm, and confusion about managing project scope effectively.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Create engaging content (blogs, webinars) that highlights the importance of scope management in open source projects; leverage social media to reach developers.
- **Metric:** Number of website visits and engagement on awareness content (e.g., blog views, social media shares).

## Consider
- **Trigger Event:** Developers start researching solutions to manage project scope after experiencing burnout.
- **Friction Points:** Overwhelming number of tools available; difficulty in finding a solution that specifically addresses open source needs.
- **User Emotions:** Skepticism, cautious optimism, and anxiety about choosing the right tool.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Provide clear comparisons with existing tools, testimonials from other open source maintainers, and case studies demonstrating effectiveness.
- **Metric:** Number of demo requests and downloads of product literature.

## Try
- **Trigger Event:** Developers decide to try Scope Shield after evaluating it against other options.
- **Friction Points:** Initial setup complexity; learning curve associated with new tools.
- **User Emotions:** Hopeful but anxious about the effectiveness of the tool; potential frustration during setup.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Offer an easy onboarding process with guided tutorials, responsive customer support, and a community forum for peer assistance.
- **Metric:** Number of active users during the trial period and completion rate of onboarding tutorials.

## Adopt
- **Trigger Event:** Developers successfully integrate Scope Shield into their workflow and see initial positive results.
- **Friction Points:** Resistance to changing established workflows; potential bugs or issues during initial usage.
- **User Emotions:** Relief and satisfaction from managing scope effectively; cautious excitement about the tool's potential.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Regular check-ins with users to gather feedback, provide tips for maximizing tool usage, and highlight success stories from the community.
- **Metric:** User retention rate and frequency of tool usage.

## Expand
- **Trigger Event:** Developers recognize the value of Scope Shield and consider additional features or upgrades.
- **Friction Points:** Uncertainty about the value of premium features; potential budget constraints for open source projects.
- **User Emotions:** Enthusiasm about expanding capabilities; concern about costs.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Offer tiered pricing models, showcase advanced features through webinars, and provide discounts or grants for open source projects.
- **Metric:** Conversion rate to premium features and customer lifetime value (CLV).
